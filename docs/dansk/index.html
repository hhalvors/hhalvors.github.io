F.C. Sibbern (1785–1872)

Frederik Christian Sibbern, professor of philosophy at Copenhagen 1813–1870. Close friend of Poul Martin Møller; Kierkegaard attended his lectures. A transitional figure who resisted the Hegelian campaign of the 1830s and anticipated several of Kierkegaard's central arguments.

Menneskets aandelige Natur og Væsen. Et Udkast til en Psychologie
1819–1828. Copenhagen
Sibbern's principal psychological work, published in two parts: Part 1 (1819) and Part 2 (1828), the latter subtitled »Psychologisk Pathologie«. Part 2 was reissued 1885 by his son Gabriel Sibbern as »Læren om de menneskelige Følelser og Lidenskaber«.

Poul Martin Møller (1794–1838)

Poet, philosopher, and Professor of Philosophy at Copenhagen 1831–1838. The most important philosophical figure in the generation immediately preceding Kierkegaard; the Concept of Anxiety is dedicated to his memory.

Søren Kierkegaard (1813–1855)

The essential upstream figure for the entire Nielsen–Brøchner–Høffding debate. This collection includes only passages most directly relevant to that debate; complete texts are available through the Royal Danish Library and in the Princeton University Press Kierkegaard's Writings series.

Rasmus Nielsen (1809–1884)

Professor of philosophy at Copenhagen 1841–1884. Developed the central position of the mid-century debate: that faith and knowledge spring from absolutely heterogeneous principles. Target of both Brøchner's philosophical critique and Brandes's cultural polemic.

Hans Brøchner (1820–1875)

Professor of philosophy at Copenhagen 1857–1875. Post-Hegelian, moved toward a naturalistic humanism under the influence of Feuerbach and Strauss. His critical engagement with Kierkegaard and Nielsen makes him a crucial third voice; Høffding credited Brøchner's lectures as the direct cause of his own conversion away from Nielsen's position.

Om Søren Kierkegaards Virksomhed som religieus Forfatter
1855. Fædrelandet, 1 December 1855
Published anonymously under the mark "-r". Brøchner's first comprehensive account of Kierkegaard's authorship, written just after Kierkegaard's death. Article text reprinted in C.H. Koch (ed.), Brøchner og Kierkegaard (Scientia Danica, Series H, Humanistica 8, vol. 13).

Harald Høffding (1843–1931)

Nielsen's student and eventual successor as the dominant figure in Danish philosophy. His intellectual development is the key to the whole debate: in 1866 he explicitly defended Nielsen's position, then within a year — under Brøchner's influence — changed his mind completely, spending the next three decades working out what that conversion implied.

David Favrholdt (1931–2012)

Dr.phil. and professor of philosophy at the University of Southern Denmark. As a student he heard Niels Bohr lecture on quantum mechanics and atomic physics, an encounter that shaped his philosophical work for the rest of his life. The leading Danish interpreter of Bohr's philosophy of complementarity and of the epistemological limits set by language and concepts.

Filosoffen Niels Bohr
2009. Informations Forlag, Copenhagen
Favrholdt's synthesis of his lifelong study of Bohr as thinker, arguing that Bohr was a genuine philosopher whose insight into quantum mechanics yielded a modern understanding of the limits of thought imposed by our language and concepts. A clean modern e-book (epub) edition exists, so this is a translation-only project — no transcription needed.
