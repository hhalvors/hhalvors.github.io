\begin{frame}{Background }

  \begin{enumerate}
  \item Einstein had been dissatisfied with QM for about fifteen
    years.
    \begin{enumerate}
    \item Failure of determinism (quantum jumps)
    \item Failure to explain in terms of local causes
    \end{enumerate}
  \item Einstein had tried several times earlier to show that the
    uncertainy relations do not hold in principle. 
  \item Bohr had proposed the notion of \emph{complementarity}.
  \end{enumerate}

\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{The argument's structure}

COM: The description of reality given by the wavefunction in
quantum mechanics is complete.

DET: $Q$ and $P$ can have simultaneous reality.

\bigskip \begin{tabular}{l c l} 
 Given from QM: & & COM $\Longrightarrow$ $\neg$DET \\ 
 Argument by EPR: & & COM $\Longrightarrow$ DET \end{tabular}

         
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Completeness and the EE link}

Eigenstate-Eigenvalue link: a quantity $A$ has a value in state
$\varphi$ iff $\varphi$ is an eigenstate for $A$.

The EE link is a way of cashing out ``the description of reality given
by the wavefunction.''


\end{frame}
