The Modern Breakthrough in Scandinavia

Course information

Term: Spring 2026
Course numbers: HUM 424 / ART 424 / PHI 423

Instructors:
- Bridget Alsdorf
- Hans P. Halvorson

Enrollment: By application or interview. Departmental permission required.

Distribution area: EM or LA
Grading basis: NA, NPDF

Description

This course explores the intersecting innovations of philosophy, art, science, and social politics that defined the Modern Breakthrough, a period of dramatic transformation in Scandinavia spanning the late nineteenth and early twentieth centuries.

Rather than treating these developments in isolation, the course takes a deliberately interdisciplinary approach. Students will examine how new artistic movements, philosophical ideas, scientific worldviews, and political reforms mutually informed and reshaped one another during a decisive moment in the history of ideas. Topics may include realism and symbolism in literature and art, the emergence of modern psychology and social theory, transformations in conceptions of nature and the human subject, and the cultural and political conditions that made these changes possible.

Study trip to Denmark

A central component of the course is a required study trip to Denmark during Spring Break. The trip will include extended visits to Copenhagen and Skagen, allowing students to engage directly with the geographical, artistic, and intellectual contexts of the Modern Breakthrough. Planned activities include visits to museums, archives, architectural sites, and landscapes associated with key figures and movements studied in the course, as well as guided discussions connecting the historical material to our seminar themes.

This immersive experience is designed to complement the seminar readings and discussions by situating them within the cultural environments in which they originally emerged.

In addition to the study trip, the course includes visits to the Princeton University Art Museum and regular seminar-style discussion centered on primary texts and visual materials.
