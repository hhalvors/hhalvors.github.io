Kierkegaard in Denmark

Day Excursion: Lejre & Roskilde

Theme: From Heroic Paganism to Christendom

Departure & Return: Copenhagen
Total Duration: ~8–9 hours

Overview

This excursion traces the historical and symbolic arc of Denmark:

  • Lejre → Mythic kings, heroic pagan culture
  • Roskilde → Christian monarchy and state church

The day frames Kierkegaard’s critique of “Christendom” against the deeper historical layers of Danish identity.

8:30 — Depart Copenhagen

Bus Reading (30–40 minutes)

Assigned Texts:

  1. Fear and Trembling, Preface + Exordium
    (The knight of faith and the solitary individual)

  2. Optional short excerpt from The Present Age
    (On leveling and loss of passion)

Students should mark passages that emphasize: - Heroism - Individual decision - Passion - The contrast between greatness and modern mediocrity

Stop 1: Lejre — Myth & Heroic Culture

10:00–12:30

Location: Viking Museum / Sagnlandet Lejre
Sagnlandet Lejre

Lejre is traditionally associated with the Skjoldung kings and early Danish mythic history.

Orientation Talk (15 minutes)

Themes:

  • Pre-Christian heroic ethos
  • Honor, fate, bloodline
  • Public greatness vs. inward faith
  • Sacrifice in pagan vs. Christian frameworks

Framing Question:

Is Abraham closer to a pagan hero — or something entirely different?

Discussion Prompts (Lejre)

  1. What distinguishes the knight of faith from a tragic hero?
  2. Does heroic pagan culture understand sacrifice differently?
  3. Is Kierkegaard romanticizing an earlier age?
  4. Does Christianity deepen or dissolve heroism?

Encourage comparison between: - Viking warrior culture
- Abraham
- Modern Danish society

12:30 — Lunch in Lejre

Preferably rustic or traditional fare.

Encourage informal conversation — no structured discussion.

Stop 2: Roskilde — Christian Monarchy & Christendom

2:00–4:30

Roskilde Cathedral

Roskilde Cathedral is burial site of Danish monarchs — monumental expression of Christianity fused with state power.

Short Orientation (10 minutes)

Themes:

  • Christianity as state religion
  • Church and monarchy
  • Public religion
  • Monumental faith

Framing Question:

Is this what Kierkegaard means by “Christendom”?

Cathedral Reading (15 minutes, silent)

Assigned Text:

  • Attack Upon Christendom, No. 1
    (The difference between Christianity and Christendom)

or

  • Practice in Christianity, section on offense

Students read quietly inside the cathedral (if appropriate) or just outside.

Discussion Prompts (Roskilde)

  1. What happens when Christianity becomes national identity?
  2. Can faith survive institutional success?
  3. Is Kierkegaard attacking the church — or complacency?
  4. Does monumental architecture strengthen or weaken inwardness?

4:45 — Depart Roskilde

Reflection Assignment (Due Next Morning)

1–2 pages:

Compare Lejre and Roskilde.
Does Christianity transform heroism — or replace it?
Where would Kierkegaard feel more at home?

Students must reference at least one passage from: - Fear and Trembling - Attack Upon Christendom (or Practice in Christianity)

Pedagogical Arc

Location Symbolic Meaning Kierkegaardian Question
Lejre Pagan heroism What is sacrifice?
Roskilde Institutional Christianity What is Christendom?

The movement of the day:

Heroic greatness
→ National Christianity
→ Kierkegaard’s critique

Closing Question (On the Bus Back)

Is Christianity a continuation of heroism — or its end?

Let the question remain unresolved.
